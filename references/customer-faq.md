# Customer FAQ

Use this reference when answering common customer questions. Keep the tone practical, honest, and sales-engineering oriented.

## Can A Rotary Cup Head Save Powder?

Short answer:

Yes, it can help save powder on suitable automatic lines, but powder saving is not caused by the cup alone.

Explain:

- Rotary cup equipment can create a stable and controlled powder cloud.
- Powder saving depends on booth airflow, powder recovery, grounding, workpiece shape, line speed, powder quality, and parameter tuning.
- Do not promise a fixed saving percentage without test data.

## Can It Improve Groove Or Recess Coverage?

Short answer:

It may improve coverage for shallow grooves and suitable difficult areas, but deep narrow recesses still need careful process tuning.

Explain:

- Recess problems are often related to Faraday cage effect.
- High voltage can sometimes worsen Faraday areas.
- Grounding, spray angle, distance, airflow, powder feed, and line speed should be checked first.
- Rotary cup heads may help by providing a broader and more even powder cloud, but they are not a magic fix for deep cavities.

## Is Rotary Cup Better Than Spray Gun?

Short answer:

Not always. It depends on production mode and workpiece shape.

Recommend rotary cup when:

- Product types are stable.
- The line is automatic or semi-automatic.
- The customer cares about consistency, film uniformity, powder utilization, and repeatable production.

Recommend spray guns or hybrid layouts when:

- Product types change frequently.
- Shapes are complex.
- Manual touch-up is required.
- Batch size is small.

## What Information Is Needed Before Equipment Recommendation?

Ask for:

- Workpiece photos, size, material, and shape.
- Production mode: manual, automatic, reciprocator, robot, or mixed.
- Current problem: powder saving, film uniformity, recess coverage, speed, labor, or quality stability.
- Line speed and output target.
- Booth and recovery system condition.
- Existing gun/equipment setup.
- Grounding and hanger cleaning condition.
- Powder type and target film thickness.

## What Should A Customer Do Before Buying?

Recommended steps:

1. Share workpiece photos or drawings.
2. Explain current line setup and pain points.
3. Confirm whether the goal is quality, speed, powder saving, labor reduction, or automatic production.
4. Test spray representative parts if possible.
5. Compare rotary cup, conventional spray gun, and hybrid layouts based on actual results.

# Equipment Selection

Use this reference when a user asks whether a rotary cup powder coating head is suitable, or how it compares with conventional electrostatic spray guns.

## Good Fit Scenarios

Rotary cup powder coating may be a good fit when the customer needs:

- Automatic powder coating on relatively stable product types.
- Better film uniformity on large or repeatable workpieces.
- Higher powder utilization through a controlled powder cloud.
- Reduced dependence on manual spraying labor.
- Consistent output on an automatic line.
- Process repeatability for medium to high production volume.

Potential workpieces:

- Metal cabinets and enclosures.
- Profiles, frames, racks, shelves, and structural parts.
- Hardware products with repeatable shapes.
- Door/window frames and similar long parts.
- Other conductive workpieces suitable for electrostatic powder coating.

## Needs Careful Evaluation

Evaluate carefully when:

- Parts have deep recesses or strong Faraday cage areas.
- Product variety changes frequently.
- Batch size is very small.
- Existing pretreatment, grounding, or curing is unstable.
- Booth airflow or recovery system is poorly matched.
- The customer expects one equipment change to solve pretreatment or curing defects.

## Rotary Cup vs Spray Gun

Rotary cup strengths:

- Stable and broad powder cloud when properly tuned.
- Useful for automatic line consistency.
- Can help with film uniformity and powder utilization.
- Good for repeatable products and controlled production.

Conventional spray gun strengths:

- Flexible for complex shapes and frequent product changes.
- Easier for manual touch-up.
- Lower entry complexity for small batches.
- Familiar to many coating teams.

Balanced recommendation:

- Recommend rotary cup when the customer's production is repeatable and the process can be controlled.
- Recommend conventional guns or hybrid layouts when the product mix is complex or needs flexible touch-up.

## Selection Questions

Ask:

- What parts will be coated, and can the user share pictures or dimensions?
- Current coating method: manual gun, automatic gun, reciprocator, robot, or no existing line?
- Main target: powder saving, speed, film uniformity, appearance, labor reduction, or quality stability?
- Daily output and shift pattern.
- Target film thickness.
- Existing booth and recovery system.
- Country/region and support expectation.

## Recommendation Format

Use:

- Fit: suitable / maybe suitable / not the first choice.
- Reason: 2-4 practical reasons.
- Risk: what must be verified.
- Next step: test spraying, part photos, line data, or supplier consultation.

# Process Parameters

Use these values as starting ranges only. Actual settings depend on the equipment model, powder, booth airflow, part shape, line speed, grounding, and quality target.

## Parameters To Collect

Before giving a strong recommendation, collect:

- Workpiece material, size, weight, and geometry.
- Pretreatment process.
- Powder type, brand, particle size, storage condition, and reclaim ratio.
- Line speed and hanger spacing.
- Spray distance and angle.
- Voltage and current limit.
- Cup speed.
- Powder feed rate.
- Atomizing/shaping air settings if applicable.
- Booth airflow and recovery system condition.
- Grounding resistance.
- Target film thickness and quality standard.

## Tuning Order

Use this order for most problems:

1. Confirm mechanical condition: cup cleanliness, pump, hose, air supply, powder fluidization.
2. Confirm grounding and hanger cleanliness.
3. Set distance and spray angle.
4. Stabilize powder feed.
5. Tune cup speed and shaping air for pattern.
6. Tune voltage and current limit.
7. Validate film thickness and appearance on real parts.

## Practical Guidance

Grounding:

- Poor grounding can create many false symptoms, including low deposition, uneven film, unstable powder attraction, and poor edge coverage.
- Hangers should be cleaned regularly. A painted hanger can make a good spray system look bad.

Distance:

- Too close can create heavy build, uneven film, and back ionization risk.
- Too far can reduce deposition and edge coverage.
- Keep distance consistent across the workpiece path where possible.

Voltage/current:

- Higher electrostatic force can improve attraction, but too much can worsen Faraday areas, back ionization, and surface texture.
- Tune voltage and current together with distance and powder output.

Cup speed:

- Cup speed changes the powder cloud shape and distribution.
- Too low may produce poor atomization or uneven distribution.
- Too high may spread powder too widely or reduce useful deposition depending on booth and part distance.

Powder feed:

- Increase gradually.
- Too much powder can waste powder, create heavy film, and hide real coverage issues.
- Too little powder can make the operator over-correct with voltage or distance.

Line speed:

- If the line is too fast for the part geometry, parameter tuning has limited effect.
- Match spray pattern width, pass overlap, and part dwell time to the desired film build.

## Suggested Response Caution

Do not promise exact film thickness, transfer efficiency, or powder saving percentage without test data. Say "starting point", "needs test spraying", or "requires on-site validation" when appropriate.
